How to play

To start playing, press the green “Play” button found on the first page.
If you are in the middle of a game and don't like what you see, press the green button again — now labeled “Restart”.
If you have just lost the game and want to try again, press “Play Again” (yes, it's the same green button).
To return to the first page of the game, press the red “Back” button.
If you want to take a break — relax, drink a coffee, and then continue playing to beat your high score — press the orange “Pause” button to stop, and “Resume” to continue.
If you are tired of the game, simply press the blue “Logout” button.

Moving the tetrominos is simple. Once they start falling, you can move them left or right using the arrow keys on your keyboard.
To rotate a piece, press the Up arrow.
To move it down faster, press the Down arrow, or press the Space key for a hard drop.
If you have rotated a piece and it ends up too close to the wall, you won't be able to rotate it again unless you move it slightly away from the wall first.

Rules

You have to place the tetrominos to make a straight line.
You can clear 1, 2, 3, or 4 lines at the same time.
Your points depend on your level: for example, clearing 1 line at level 1 gives you 100 points, while clearing 3 lines at level 4 gives you 500 × 4 points.
Your goal is to score as many points as possible without the tetrominos reaching the top wall.
Points per line clear:
- 1 line = 100
- 2 lines = 300
- 3 lines = 500
- 4 lines = 800
If you press Space (hard drop), you will earn 1 point for every cell the piece moves down.

History of the game

Tetris was created in 1985 by Alexey Pajitnov while working at the Dorodnitsyn Computing Centre of the Soviet Academy of Sciences in Moscow. He first developed it on an Elektronika 60 computer, using tetrominoes as the basic game pieces.

The game quickly spread when Vadim Gerasimov and Dmitry Pavlovsky ported it to the IBM PC, adding colors and scoring. It circulated freely in Moscow before reaching Hungary, where Robert Stein encountered it and sought to license it internationally.

By 1987-1988, the rights became disputed between several companies, including Mirrorsoft, Spectrum HoloByte, and Atari Games. At the same time, Henk Rogers secured rights from the Soviet agency Elorg for Nintendo.

In 1989, Nintendo released Tetris for the Game Boy, bundled with the console. This version sold over 35 million copies and propelled Tetris to global fame, becoming one of the most influential video games of all time.

In 1996, the rights reverted to Pajitnov, who, alongside Rogers, founded The Tetris Company to manage and license the brand. Since then, countless official and unofficial versions have appeared across computers, consoles, and mobile devices.

In the modern era, Tetris has remained popular through titles like Tetris Effect (2018) and Tetris 99 (2019), continuing its legacy as one of the most iconic puzzle games ever created.
