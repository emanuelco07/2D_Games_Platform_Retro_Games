How to play

To start playing, press the “Space” key.
If you are in the middle of a game and don't like what you see, press the green button — labeled “Restart”.
If you have just lost the game and want to try again, press “Play Again” (yes, it's the same green button).
To return to the first page of the game, press the red “Back” button.
If you want to take a break — relax, drink a coffee, and then continue playing to beat your high score — press the orange “Pause” button to stop, and “Resume” to continue.
If you are tired of the game, simply press the blue “Logout” button.

Playing the game is very simple:
You have to move the paddle left or right (using the Left and Right keys) so that the ball will collide with the bricks.
You earn a certain number of points for every brick you break, depending on your level.
You can move the ball faster by pressing the Space key.

Rules

You have to destroy as many bricks as possible, or clear all of them without losing any lives, which means keeping the ball from falling below the paddle.

History of the game

Brick Breaker is a classic breakout clone video game where the player controls a paddle to bounce a ball and break bricks at the top of the screen.

The genre began with Atari's Breakout in 1976, designed by Nolan Bushnell and Steve Bristow. It inspired numerous successors and clones throughout the arcade and home console era.

Brick Breaker itself became widely popular in the 2000s after being preloaded on BlackBerry mobile phones, turning it into one of the most iconic mobile games of its time.

Many variations introduced power-ups such as multi-ball, laser paddles, and extended paddles, adding new layers of strategy and replayability.

The game has remained influential in gaming culture, inspiring modern adaptations and mobile versions long after its original release.
